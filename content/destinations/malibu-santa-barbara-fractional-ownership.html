What is fractional ownership in real estate?

Fractional ownership in real estate means co-owning a vacation property with a small group of buyers, where each owner holds a deeded share (typically 1/8th). Unlike timeshares, fractional ownership gives you actual property ownership, shared equity, and usage rights of 6 weeks per year. All maintenance, management, and costs are professionally handled and split among co-owners. This model is ideal for those seeking luxury vacation homes without the full commitment and expense of sole ownership. Also, as your name is on the property deed, you can transfer it to your children for the property to stay in the family for generations. For Europeans, this is very advantageous, as your children will pay inheritance tax on only 1/8 of the property’s value, yet they will retain use of the full property when they stay there.

What is the difference between Malibu and Santa Barbara?

Malibu is closer to Los Angeles (30-40 minutes), famous for its 21 miles of scenic coastline, “surf chic” lifestyle, and celebrity beach culture. Malibu feels more raw and exclusive—think beachfront compounds with private gates, rooftop decks overlooking surf breaks, and the casual sophistication of dining at Nobu or shopping at Malibu Country Mart. It’s perfect for buyers who want direct beach access, world-class surfing, and the authentic Southern California beach lifestyle with proximity to LA’s entertainment industry.

Santa Barbara & Montecito are 90 minutes north of LA, offering a more polished, European-inspired atmosphere with Spanish Colonial architecture, a vibrant walkable downtown, and a slower, more refined pace of life. Montecito specifically attracts buyers seeking extreme privacy (celebrity residents like Oprah), aristocratic elegance, and Mediterranean sophistication. Santa Barbara provides better walkability, proximity to wine country (45 minutes), and a more family-friendly, residential vibe compared to Malibu’s beach scene.

We offer fractional properties in both regions, allowing you to choose between Malibu’s surf culture and beachfront exclusivity or Santa Barbara/Montecito’s European sophistication and privacy.
